Observability plot:

The green curve shows the altitude of the moon. The solid blue curve shows the object's altitude for the starting date of observations. The dashed blue curve shows the object's altitude for the last date of observations. The light shaded region indicates the twilight (sun altitude < 0°). The dark shaded region indicates the night time (sun altitude < -18°). The brown horizontal dashed line indicates the 30° altitude limit. The vertical black dashed lines (when present) indicate the times at which the object crosses the 30° altitude limit (on the starting date). 

Signal to Noise Ratio (SNR):

We have taken into account the source's shot noise, the detector's noise and the background noise (i.e. light pollution) in estimating the SNR. The latter is derived from the Sky Quality Meter readings of the respective site [1].
