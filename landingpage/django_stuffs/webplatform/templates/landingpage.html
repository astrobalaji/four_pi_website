Professional Astronomer

What do you gain by joining our network?

1

Easy access to high time resolution data

With our global network of amateur astronomers, you can have easy and affordable access to high time resolution photometric data. 

2

Instant observation requests

Tired of waiting for observation cycles of different telescopes? With our global network, you can request data at any time you wish. 

3

Expand your research outreach

Alongside your scientific observations, you can share your expertise in the field of your research with the amateur astronomers and students across the planet. 

Amateur Astronomer

1

Participate in cutting edge research

Be a part of a new astronomical discovery or help  in answering key questions about the universe. With our network you can join hands with a professional astronomer by collecting astronomical data. All you need is a telescope, tracker mount and a camera. 

2

Earn while you do your favorite hobby

Based on the amount of data that you contribute in the network, you will be rewarded with monetary and other benefits.

What are we planning?

What can you expect from us?

A community of citizen scientists

Webplatform where Amateur Astronomers, Professional Astronomers and Educators can network together to learn and contribute to cutting edge research. 

A new age telescope

Its time to change the fundamental design of optical telescopes. We will soon release our own unique smart telescopes with our new age non-glass based optics. They are light, rigid (almost unbreakable optics), thermally stable and affordable with high optical quality on the images.  

semi-robotic backyard observatories

We have developed a cheaper semi-robotic smart observatory setup for your backyard. It is easy to install with a few steps. 

Astrotourism

A network of semi-robotic telescopes places in unique darksites. Where you can visit with your loved ones to enjoy the beautiful night sky and at the same time contribute data to scientific research.

Network of Space telescopes 

A network of space telescopes in orbit, which can be built locally in space using our new age optics.
